<p><p>The cystic fibrosis diagnostic tests market encompasses various testing methods. Genetic tests identify mutations in the CFTR gene, confirming cystic fibrosis. Fecal tests assess pancreatic function by measuring fat absorption. The immunoreactive trypsinogen (IRT) test detects elevated trypsinogen levels in newborns, indicating potential cystic fibrosis. Sweat chloride tests measure chloride levels in sweat; high levels suggest the disease. Lastly, pulmonary function tests evaluate lung capacity and function, monitoring disease progression and treatment efficacy. Together, these tests facilitate early diagnosis and management of cystic fibrosis.</p></p>
