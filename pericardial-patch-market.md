<p><ul><li>Autologous</li><li>Xenograft</li><li>Synthetic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Pericardial patches are used in cardiac surgery to repair or reconstruct the pericardium. There are three main types of pericardial patches available in the market: autologous, xenograft, and synthetic. Autologous patches are derived from the patient's own tissue, providing a natural and compatible option. Xenograft patches are sourced from animal tissue, typically porcine or bovine, and are widely used due to their availability and biocompatibility. Synthetic patches are made from materials such as expanded polytetrafluoroethylene (ePTFE) or polyester, offering durability and flexibility.</p></p>
