<p><p>Educational trinocular microscopes come in two main types: inverted and upright. Inverted microscopes have the light source and condenser located above the specimen, making them ideal for viewing cells and tissue cultures in containers. Upright microscopes have the light source and condenser located below the specimen, making them better for viewing larger, solid samples like minerals and insects. Both types offer trinocular viewing, allowing for simultaneous viewing through eyepieces and camera systems, making them versatile tools for educational and research purposes.</p></p>
