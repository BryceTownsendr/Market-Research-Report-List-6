<p><p>Proximity card market is divided into three main types based on frequency: Low Frequency Cards, High Frequency Cards, and Ultra-High Frequency Cards. Low Frequency Cards operate at a frequency of 125 kHz, typically used for access control and employee identification. High Frequency Cards operate at 13.56 MHz and are commonly used for payment systems and transit passes. Ultra-High Frequency Cards operate at frequencies above 800 MHz, offering longer read ranges and faster data transfer speeds, making them ideal for tracking and inventory management applications.</p></p>
