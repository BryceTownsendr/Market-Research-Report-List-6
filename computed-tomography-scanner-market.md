<p><p>Computed Tomography (CT) scanners are classified into three main types based on the number of slices they can generate in a single rotation: Low-slice scanners (<64 slices), Medium-slice scanners (64 slices), and High-slice scanners (>64 slices). Low-slice scanners are generally more economical and are suitable for basic imaging needs. Medium-slice scanners offer improved image quality and faster scanning times. High-slice scanners are the most advanced, providing high-resolution images with superior clarity and detail, making them ideal for complex diagnostic procedures.</p></p>
