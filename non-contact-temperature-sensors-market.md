Infrared temperature sensors detect infrared radiation, making them ideal for high-speed applications and hazardous environments. They are widely used in industrial processes, medical applications, and building diagnostics. Fiber optic temperature sensors utilize light transmission through optical fibers to measure temperature changes, offering high precision and resistance to electromagnetic interference. These sensors are suitable for harsh environments, such as chemical plants and oil refineries.</p></p>
