<p><p>The CFRP (Carbon Fiber Reinforced Polymer) recycling market comprises two primary types: chemical and physical processes. The chemical process involves breaking down CFRP into its composite components using chemical reactions, which helps recover fibers and matrix materials for reuse. Conversely, the physical process focuses on mechanically processing CFRP, utilizing methods such as grinding or shredding to produce recycled materials. Both methods aim to provide sustainable solutions for waste management in the aerospace, automotive, and construction industries.</p></p>
