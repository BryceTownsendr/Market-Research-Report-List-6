<p><p>Molecular diagnosis of hereditary colorectal cancer involves various advanced techniques to detect genetic mutations. PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) amplifies specific DNA sequences for mutation analysis. FISH (Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization) visualizes chromosomal abnormalities by tagging specific DNA probes with fluorescent dyes. DNA sequencing provides detailed insights into the nucleotide structure of genes, allowing for precise mutation identification. Gene chips analyze multiple genes simultaneously, offering a comprehensive view of genetic alterations relevant to colorectal cancer susceptibility. These methods enhance early detection and personalized treatment approaches.</p></p>
