<p><p>Fluorescent DNA dyes are essential tools in molecular biology research for visualizing DNA in gels. Ethidium Bromide, Hoechst Dye, Propidium Iodide, and other fluorescent DNA dyes are commonly used in research labs. Ethidium Bromide intercalates between DNA base pairs emitting red fluorescence, while Hoechst Dye binds to the minor groove of DNA emitting blue fluorescence. Propidium Iodide binds to DNA emitting red fluorescence. Other types of fluorescent DNA dyes offer unique properties and benefits for DNA visualization in research applications.</p></p>
