Warming systems are used to maintain the patient's body temperature during surgeries or in critical care situations by delivering warm fluids into the bloodstream. On the other hand, cooling systems are designed to lower body temperature of patients to induce therapeutic hypothermia for conditions like cardiac arrest or traumatic brain injury. Both types of systems play a crucial role in regulating patients' body temperature and improving outcomes in healthcare settings.</p></p>
