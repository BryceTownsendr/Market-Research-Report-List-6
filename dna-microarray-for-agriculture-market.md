<p><p>DNA microarrays are powerful tools in agriculture, enabling the study of gene expression and genetic variations in crops. Oligonucleotide DNA microarrays (oDNA) use short DNA sequences to hybridize with target genes, allowing for precise analysis of gene expression profiles. Complementary DNA microarrays (cDNA) are created from mRNA transcripts, providing insights into active genes in specific tissues or conditions. Both types facilitate advancements in plant breeding, disease resistance research, and understanding complex traits in agricultural species.</p></p>
