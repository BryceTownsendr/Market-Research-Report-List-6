Lubricated plug valves feature a filling of lubricant that reduces friction during operation, enhancing performance and lifespan, making them suitable for throttling services. In contrast, non-lubricated plug valves operate without additional lubrication, offering a simpler design and lower maintenance, commonly used in applications where contamination must be avoided. Both types serve critical roles in regulating fluid flow in various industries, including oil and gas, water treatment, and chemical processing.</p></p>
