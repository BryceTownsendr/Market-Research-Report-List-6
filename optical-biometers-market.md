<p><p>The optical biometer market is segmented into two main types: contact and non-contact. Contact types involve direct interaction with the eye, providing precise measurements through physical contact, which can enhance accuracy but may discomfort patients. Non-contact types use light waves to measure ocular parameters without touching the eye, offering a more comfortable experience and faster assessments. Both types play crucial roles in ophthalmology and optometry, catering to varying patient needs and clinical settings.</p></p>
